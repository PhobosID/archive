About Minecrush

Minecrush Network (MCN) is a Minecraft: Bedrock Edition Server based in Indonesia. We have tons of features, such as Survival Multiplayer, OneBlock, and SkyBlock with tons of addons!

Everyone are welcome here! Because our goal is to bring all the Minecrafters around the world to gather and join our server. We want to expand our community to the next level, and create a great community!

We started back in 4th of April 2019, with the hope that we'll be the best Minecraft BE server out there, started building a small community, we've been through ups and downs on our server, and with the help of our members & staff, we grown into one of the biggest Minecraft: Bedrock Edition community in Indonesia. and we want you to be part of our journey.

Have Any Questions?

1 Is this a Minecraft: Java Edition Server?
Unfortunately, No. This is a Minecraft: Bedrock Edition Server. We use different software from bedrock called "PocketMine".
2 Is this server able to crossplay?
Unfortunately, No. We don't use Geyser as other server did. But don't worry, we'll consider to add that in the future.
3 What is CE and how can I get it?
CE (Custom-Enchant) is an enchant that can be applied to your tools, weapons, or armors. The enchant is unique and more powerful than vanilla enchantment. To get this, you need to purchase at least Sentinel Rank in order to get one CE. Making CE is the most valuable item in this server.
4 Is this Server Keep-Inventory and Keep-XP?
Fret not! Our Server is Keep-Inventory and Keep-XP. Meaning If you die, you won't lose any items in your backpack, and also you won't lose your XP.
5 How can I claim land in the Survival Economy?
We Use 3 commands to claim land. Claim Land cost money. You can earn money at the Mine. The first step to do is to go to first corner, then type /startp. Second is to go to second corner, then type /endp. Finally, type /land buy to buy the desired claim. The Claim Process itself is so simmilar to the GriefPrevention in Java Edition. But in here, we use /startp and /endp.
